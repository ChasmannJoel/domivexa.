1. Introduction

This Cookie Policy explains how Domivexa Ltd ("we," "our," or "us") uses cookies and similar technologies on our website domivexa.co.uk. This policy should be read in conjunction with our Privacy Policy.

2. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, smartphone, or tablet) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to website owners.

Cookies can be:

  • Session Cookies: Temporary cookies that expire when you close your browser
  • Persistent Cookies: Cookies that remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them
  • First-Party Cookies: Set by the website you're visiting
  • Third-Party Cookies: Set by a different website than the one you're visiting

4. Third-Party Cookies

We use services from third parties that may set their own cookies. These include:

4.1 Google Analytics

We use Google Analytics to understand how visitors use our website. Google Analytics uses cookies to collect anonymous information about visitor behavior. You can opt out of Google Analytics by installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

4.2 Social Media Platforms

If you interact with social media features on our website (e.g., Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn), these platforms may set their own cookies. We do not control these cookies.

4.3 Advertising Networks

We may use advertising networks to display relevant ads based on your browsing behavior. These networks may use cookies to track your interests across different websites.

5. Managing Cookies

You have several options to manage and control cookies:

5.1 Cookie Banner

When you first visit our website, you'll see a cookie banner allowing you to accept or reject non-essential cookies.

5.2 Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can:

  • View what cookies are stored and delete them individually
  • Block third-party cookies
  • Block cookies from specific websites
  • Block all cookies
  • Delete all cookies when you close your browser

Learn how to manage cookies in popular browsers:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

5.3 Third-Party Opt-Out Tools

You can opt out of targeted advertising through:

  • Your Online Choices (for EU/UK users)
  • Network Advertising Initiative
  • Digital Advertising Alliance

6. Impact of Disabling Cookies

Please note that disabling cookies may affect your experience on our website:

  • Some features may not work properly
  • You may need to re-enter information or preferences
  • Some pages may not display correctly
  • We won't be able to remember your cookie preferences

Essential cookies are necessary for the website to function, and disabling them may prevent you from using certain features.

7. Web Beacons and Pixels

In addition to cookies, we may use web beacons (also known as tracking pixels) in our website and emails. These are small graphic files that allow us to monitor user activity and track email campaign effectiveness. They work in conjunction with cookies to collect information about website usage and email engagement.

8. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers have a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature that signals websites you visit that you do not want to be tracked. Currently, there is no industry standard for how to respond to DNT signals. We do not currently respond to DNT signals, but we provide you with control over cookies through our cookie banner and browser settings.
